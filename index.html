Welcome to Scala Puzzlers, a central repository of Traps, Pitfalls and Corner Cases in Scala language.

Prepare to be surprised, entertained and...well, puzzled! What is presented here is a selection of seemingly simple examples which demonstrate that there's plenty of head-scratching left in Scala! Let your mind be challenged by unexpected and unintuitive behaviour and results and learn something in the process.

All puzzlers are conveniently listed on the left side of the page.

Your contributions are welcomed

Have a puzzler you would like to share with the rest of the community? Please follow these instructions in order to get it included to the repository. And don't worry, you will get properly creditted for your contribtuion!

We would love to hear from you

Any kind of feedback is appreciated, including the ones about this website but also about the puzzlers themselves. If there is something you would like to let us know, please contact us.
